MOST RECENT WORK

COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS

Assess the strengths and weaknesses of competitors against your user's needs

ESTABLISH EMPATHY

See the problem from the perspective of the user through contextual inquiry to identify the key goals and motivations that will make up each persona

CONTENT STRATEGY

Pinpoint why this content is needed from a business and user perspective, what is the tone, who is our audience, where will the content appear, how will it be presented, and when will it be published

INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE

Through card sorting and journey mapping, create a hierarchical diagram of your content grouping that may take the form of taxonomies or site maps

USER FLOW

Map the users journey through the content via task analysis and user flow diagrams to identify any roadblocks of disconnects

WIREFRAMING

Visually communicate abstract concepts with users and team members to generate feedback"

RAPID PROTOTYPING

Work hand in hand with users and stakeholders to generate interactive mockups of the product

HIGH FIDELITY MOCKUPS

Apply product branding, typography, color scheme and icons

HIGH FIDELITY PROTOTYPES

Create interactive prototypes with Omnigrafle or InVision. This allows the team to validate the product through usability testing before it is built

I started my professional career as an architect in the center of silicon valley, surrounded by its booming industry. I instantly began to see the parrallels between user experience design and architecture. In both, the most critical skill was empathy. Understanding who will be using what you create, who will give it purpose. I firmly believe in this mindset and the key to implementing it lies in the
process.

I am a recent grad looking for the opportunity to hone these
ideas into a road tested methodology. The only way to do this

User experience will force me to continuously validate my
